What is Mallo?

The first analytics site for pesäpallo (Finnish baseball). English, because the ideas came from the baseball and basketball analytics world — Finnish UI everywhere else.

Public analytics transformed how baseball (Baseball Savant, FanGraphs, Baseball-Reference) and basketball (DARKO, LEBRON) are understood — but pesäpallo, a sport with a century of history and a results service that records every plate turn, has never had an analytics layer. Mallo is an attempt to build one, on top of the official pesistulokset.fi data service.

PARE (Painotettu ja Regressoitu Ennuste — "weighted and regressed projection"; methodologically a DARKO transplant) is a daily-updating estimate of every player's true talent in every rate stat. Every game a player has ever played counts, weighted by an exponential decay fitted per stat — no arbitrary "last 10 games" windows — and blended with the league average in proportion to how much evidence exists. Hot streak in a small sample? The model stays skeptical. Sustained improvement? It follows. Projected stats carry an e- prefix (eTEHO+, eKL% — ennustettu), park-adjusted ones a k- prefix (kTEHO+ — kenttäkorjattu): descriptive initialisms in the WAR/OPS+/xBA tradition.

TEHO+ is league-indexed production per plate turn (100 = league average) in the spirit of baseball's OPS+/wRC+ — so seasons and eras can be compared honestly. Because pesäpallo production concentrates at the top of the batting order, the league's best hitters run 250–350.

Percentile profiles (the red-to-blue sliders) show where a player ranks among qualified peers in each skill, Baseball Savant style. Kenttäkertoimet are the sport's first published park factors — the results service records weather on every match, so wind and temperature effects are measured, not folklore. Playoff odds come from Monte Carlo simulation of the remaining schedule. And the baseball translation pages map any player's league percentiles onto MLB distributions, for explaining pesäpallo performances to baseball fans.

Data: the official pesistulokset.fi API (free keys from tulospalvelu@pesis.fi), with per-player per-match statistics back to 1990. This instance may be running the built-in synthetic demo league; real-data ingest is a one-command backfill once a key is configured.

Open source, work in progress. The roadmap — run expectancy from play-by-play, lukkari metrics, manager decision analysis, win probability — lives in docs/design.md.
