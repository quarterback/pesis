Pesäpallo in 60 seconds, for baseball people

The pitch is vertical. The "pitcher" (lukkari) stands at the plate and tosses the ball straight up; hitting is about placement, not beating velocity. Think of every at-bat as a bunt-or-drive decision against a shifted defense.

The manager bats. Not literally — but the pelinjohtaja signals every swing, take, and advance with a giant fan. Strategy density is closer to football play-calling than baseball managing.

Wounds aren't outs. A caught fly "wounds" (haavoittaa) the advancing runner — he's erased, but no out is recorded. Outs (palot, "burns") happen when the ball beats a runner to the base.

A kärkilyönti — advancing the lead runner — is the core batting skill, the sport's batting average. Home runs (kunnarit) exist but never leave the park: the batter sprints the bases like an inside-the-park homer, every time.

Structure: two 4-inning periods; win both, win the game. Split them and you play a super-inning (extra innings), then a one-on-one homer derby (kotiutuslyöntikilpailu) — yes, the sport settles ties with a shootout.

The season is a sprint. 28–33 regular-season games, roughly one MLB month — so every series matters, and context matters more than in a 162-game grind: who you played, in which park, in what wind. The results service records all of it, which is why these numbers can be park- and weather-adjusted instead of taken raw.
